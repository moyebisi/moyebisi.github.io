Mental Health Analysis Dashboard

An analysis of a mental health dataset exploring how factors such as occupation, gender, self-employment, and mental health history relate to mood swings, stress levels, work interest, and social weakness across different demographics and countries. The dataset was cleaned from 287,162 rows down to 21,208 unique records before building five pivot-driven charts into a single dashboard.

Microsoft Excel PivotTables Data Cleaning Dashboard Design Data Visualization

Key Insights

  • –Housewives and Corporate workers report the highest growing stress levels
  • –Males report significantly higher mental health history than females
  • –People not in self-employment experience higher mood swings
  • –A mental health history correlates with lower work interest
  • –Canada, UK, and USA report the highest social weakness among the 16 countries analysed

Note: other major countries ranked within the same level of social weakness were excluded from the chart for clarity.

Music & Mental Health Dashboard

An exploration of whether a person's favourite music genre relates to their mental health — specifically anxiety, depression, insomnia, and OCD levels. Using survey data from 736 respondents across 16 music genres, this project identifies patterns between listening habits and self-reported mental health scores.

Microsoft Excel PivotTables Data Cleaning Dashboard Design Data Visualization

Key Insights

  • –Folk music listeners report the highest average anxiety levels while Latin listeners report the lowest
  • –Lofi music listeners report the highest average depression levels while Gospel listeners report the lowest
  • –Lofi and Gospel listeners report the highest insomnia levels while Rap listeners report the lowest
  • –Gospel music consistently shows the lowest mental health challenges across all four indicators
